Warm-Up

5–10 minutes — off the machine

  • Arm circles — 1 minute forward, 1 minute backward. Loosens shoulders and upper back.
  • Bodyweight squats — 15 slow reps. Warms up hips, knees and quads.
  • Hip circles — 10 each direction. Mobilises the hip joints.
  • Gentle torso twists — 20 reps. Prepares the spine and obliques.

Cool-Down & Stretching

10 minutes — end of every session

  • Stretcher (#7) — Sit facing column, legs on board. Reach forward slowly, hold 5 seconds × 5. Targets: Hamstrings, lower back.
  • Groin Stretch (#35) — Heel to heel, back straight, reach forward gently. Hold 10 seconds × 3. Targets: Groin, hips, hamstrings.
  • Chest Stretch (off machine) — Clasp hands behind back, draw shoulder blades together. Hold 30 seconds. Targets: Chest, front shoulders.
  • Shoulder rolls & neck tilts — Slow, controlled for 1 minute. Releases tension in neck and upper traps.

Important Safety & Training Tips

Consult Your GPAlways seek medical advice before starting a new exercise programme.
Grip Handles FirstWhen cables are hooked, always grasp handles before getting on or off the glideboard.
Control the BoardMaintain control of the glideboard at all times — never let it slide freely.
Rest Days MatterMuscles grow during recovery. Rest days are just as important as sessions.
Normal vs Sharp PainSome soreness in weeks 1–2 is normal. Sharp or joint pain is not — stop and seek advice.
Stay HydratedDrink water before, during and after every session.
Check EquipmentInspect cables and pulleys for wear before each use. Use on a solid, level surface only.
After 8 WeeksProgress to the Intermediate Programme detailed in your Total Gym 1000 manual.
